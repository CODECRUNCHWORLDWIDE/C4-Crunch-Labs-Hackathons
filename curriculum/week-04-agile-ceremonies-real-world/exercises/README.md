# Week 4 — Exercises

Three short rehearsals, done Tuesday through Friday. None of them require teammates — though all three are sharper with a partner. They are the dry-runs of the three muscles you bring to the next event: the 60-second stand-up update, the planning-poker round, and the three-sentence "no" script.

## Index

1. **[Exercise 1 — The 60-second stand-up](exercise-01-60-second-standup.md)** — draft and dry-run your stand-up update; time it; commit it. (~60 min, Tuesday)
2. **[Exercise 2 — Planning-poker round](exercise-02-pointing-poker.md)** — point a 10-item backlog with the 1-2-3-5 scale in 10 minutes. (~60 min, Thursday)
3. **[Exercise 3 — The "saying no" script](exercise-03-saying-no-script.md)** — write your three-sentence "no" for each of the three scope-creep shapes. (~60 min, Friday)

## How to work the exercises

- Read the prompt. Skim, do not memorize.
- **Type or write the answer yourself.** Do not paste a giant snippet and edit. The point is muscle memory.
- Use the C4 voice: team-room, blameless, scope-discipline-first. No "rockstar" / "ninja" / "10x." Even when you are alone.
- Commit each completed exercise to the *Week 4 prep* folder of your `hackathon-template` repo (or a new `c4-week-04-prep-<yourhandle>` repo). Small commits.
- If you get stuck for more than 15 minutes, re-read the lecture section the exercise references, *then* try again.

The three drills are short on purpose. The skill is built in the dry-run, not in the writing. Run each exercise out loud at least once before you commit it. The vocal cords catch what the eyes skip.

These exercises are the difference between a team contract written in theory and a team contract written from real reps. Do not skip them.
