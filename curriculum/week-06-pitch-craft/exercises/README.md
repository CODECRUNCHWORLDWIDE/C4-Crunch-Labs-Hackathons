# Week 6 — Exercises

Three short rehearsals, done Tuesday through Friday. None of them require teammates — though all three are sharper with a partner who watches and gives one specific note. They are the dry-runs of the three muscles you bring to the next event's demo session: writing a hook that lands, recording a first take on the live URL, and re-recording after watching yourself once.

## Index

1. **[Exercise 1 — Write the hook](exercise-01-write-the-hook.md)** — draft three candidate hooks for your Week-3 solo build, pick one, read each aloud, commit. (~60 min, Tuesday)
2. **[Exercise 2 — Record v1](exercise-02-record-v1.md)** — record the first three-minute take against the live URL, watch it four times, write three notes. (~60 min, Thursday)
3. **[Exercise 3 — Record v2 improved](exercise-03-record-v2-improved.md)** — apply the three notes, re-record, watch, compare the two takes against the strip. (~60 min, Friday)

## How to work the exercises

- Read the prompt. Skim, do not memorize.
- **Speak or write the answer yourself.** Do not paste a giant snippet and edit. The point is muscle memory — and for pitch craft, muscle memory is in the mouth, not the keyboard.
- Use the C4 voice: team-room, blameless, scope-discipline-first. No "rockstar" / "ninja" / "10x." Even when you are alone in front of a camera.
- Commit each completed exercise to the *Week 6 prep* folder of your `hackathon-template` repo (or a new `c4-week-06-prep-<yourhandle>` repo). Small commits. Video files do not commit; commit the *link* to the hosted recording instead.
- If you get stuck for more than 15 minutes, re-read the lecture section the exercise references, *then* try again.

The three drills are short on purpose. The skill is built in the recording, not in the writing. Run each exercise against a *real clock* — the 3-minute take is a hard timebox; the four watches are a hard timebox; the three-note rule is a hard cap. Honor them.

These exercises are the difference between a demo script written in theory and a demo script delivered from real reps. Do not skip them — and specifically, do not skip the *watching* part of Exercise 2. The cringe is the data.
