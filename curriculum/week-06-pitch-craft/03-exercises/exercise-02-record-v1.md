# Exercise 2 — Record v1

**Goal:** Record the first three-minute take of your Week-3 demo against the live URL. One take, no restarts. Then watch it four times, and write three notes that will drive Friday's re-record. By the end of the exercise, you have a hosted take-1 recording, four watch logs, and three structural notes — not ten wording notes.

**Estimated time:** 60 minutes (5 min setup + 5 min recording + 12 min watching + 15 min notes + 23 min hosting and commit).

---

## Why this exercise exists

Lecture 2 §1 said it: the first take of any demo is bad. Always. The first take is the *data* — it is where you discover the script you wrote does not match the script you read aloud. Most learners try to record their best take on take 1 and then quit when it does not land; the defense is a *one-take, no-restart* recording that lets the mistakes happen on purpose. The mistakes are the lesson.

The "cringe is the data" rule is the test. If you cannot sit through the four watches of your own first take, you cannot improve it. The discomfort is the cost of admission; pay it.

---

## What to produce

A file at `week-06-prep/exercise-02-record-v1.md` with:

- The hosted link to your take-1 recording (YouTube unlisted, Loom public, or Vimeo free).
- The four pre-recording check results (all four green before recording).
- The take-1 timing data — measured length of each beat against the strip.
- Four watch logs (1.0x audio+video, 1.5x audio+video, 1.0x video-only, 1.0x audio-only).
- Three structural notes for take 2 — *not* five, *not* ten.

The recording itself is hosted externally and linked in the file. Do not commit the video to the repo this week.

## Step-by-step

### 1. Run the four pre-recording checks

Open `exercise-02-record-v1.md`. Run the four checks from Lecture 2 §2.1 and log the results:

```
PRE-RECORDING CHECKS — take 1

CHECK 1 — Live URL reachable.
  URL: <paste deploy URL>
  Load time: <seconds>     (target: under 3)
  MUST verb works: yes / no
  Result: PASS / FAIL

CHECK 2 — Script ready.
  Script location: <printed sheet, second monitor, second tab>
  Word count: <number>     (target: ~415 words)
  Result: PASS / FAIL

CHECK 3 — Recording tool tested.
  Tool: <Loom / OBS / QuickTime / Game Bar / phone>
  10-second test clip recorded: yes / no
  Audio clear: yes / no
  Video crisp: yes / no
  Result: PASS / FAIL

CHECK 4 — Room quiet.
  Door closed, fan low, phone on silent, others notified: yes / no
  Twenty minutes blocked: yes / no
  Result: PASS / FAIL
```

If any check fails, fix it before pressing record. The fix is mechanical and takes under 5 minutes; do not record into a failing check.

### 2. Open the script and the live URL

Open the script (from Exercise 1 + your in-progress full script) where you can glance at it without it being in the recording. Open the live URL in a fresh browser tab on the screen you will record. Confirm the URL is on the screen, not the script.

### 3. Press record. Run the full take.

Start the recording tool. Wait 5 seconds (let any preamble settle). Begin with the picked hook from Exercise 1. Run the full 3 minutes:

- 0:00–0:30 — hook (from Exercise 1)
- 0:30–1:15 — problem beat
- 1:15–2:45 — solution beat (3 clicks on the live URL)
- 2:45–3:00 — ask

**Do not stop mid-take.** Do not restart at the apology. Do not re-record the hook because the first sentence stumbled. Let the take run. The fluffs are the lesson.

Exception: a technical failure (the live URL goes down, the recording tool crashes, the audio dropped). Then restart, log the failure, run the take again. Not for human fluffs.

### 4. Stop recording. Export. Host.

Stop the recording. Export to MP4 or upload directly to your chosen host. The C4 default is YouTube unlisted; Loom public is acceptable for take 1; Vimeo free is acceptable.

Paste the hosted link into `exercise-02-record-v1.md`:

```
TAKE-1 HOSTED LINK

URL: <paste host URL>
Host: <YouTube unlisted / Loom public / Vimeo free / other>
Recorded at: <YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM>
Take duration: <minutes:seconds>     (target: 3:00; first-take typical: 3:30-3:50)
```

If the take is longer than 4:00, log it honestly. The over-run is the data. Take 2 will trim it.

### 5. The four watches — 12 minutes total

Now you watch. The four watches from Lecture 2 §4.1, in this order:

```
WATCH 1 — 1.0x, audio on, video on (3 minutes)

Beat lengths (measure with the video timeline):
  Hook:     <m:ss>   (target 0:30)
  Problem:  <m:ss>   (target 0:45)
  Solution: <m:ss>   (target 1:30)
  Ask:      <m:ss>   (target 0:15)
  Total:    <m:ss>   (target 3:00)

Filler count: <number of "um," "like," "so," "you know," etc.>

Worst-performing beat: <hook / problem / solution / ask>
  Why: <one sentence>


WATCH 2 — 1.5x, audio on, video on (2 minutes)

Structural drift noted:
  - <one observation; e.g., "the problem beat repeats the hook
    instead of narrowing">
  - <one observation>

Pacing assessment: <one sentence; e.g., "the third click rushed
  past the demo clip; the visible result was on screen for 1
  second">


WATCH 3 — 1.0x, audio OFF, video on (3 minutes)

Visual audit:
  - Is the live URL visible during the solution beat? yes / no
  - Are the 3 clicks crisp? yes / no
  - Any stagnant screens (5+ sec of loading or silence)? yes / no
  - If yes, when: <timestamp>


WATCH 4 — 1.0x, audio on, video OFF (3 minutes)

Audio audit:
  - Does the hook land in the first 8 seconds? yes / no
  - Does the ask name a number AND a channel? yes / no
  - Are there trailing sentences (sentences that fade)? yes / no
  - Most common filler word: <word>
```

The four watches catch four different layers. Do them in order. Skipping any one of them is skipping the lesson it carries.

### 6. Write the three notes

This is the budget. *Three* notes. Not five. Not ten. Three.

```
THE THREE NOTES FOR TAKE 2

Note 1 — worst-performing beat:
  Beat: <hook / problem / solution / ask>
  One-sentence fix: <specific change you will make in take 2>

Note 2 — most common filler or trailing pattern:
  Pattern: <e.g., "starting sentences with 'so'">
  Behavioral fix: <e.g., "pause for 1 second before each sentence
    in the problem beat to break the 'so' habit">

Note 3 — structural or timing fix:
  Issue: <the beat that overran or underran by 15+ seconds>
  Fix: <specific change; e.g., "cut the second paragraph of the
    problem beat from 4 sentences to 2; reads at ~20 seconds">
```

The three notes are the *plan* for take 2. Re-read them aloud before pressing record on take 2 (Exercise 3). Awareness from the verbal re-read catches at least half of the failure modes on take 2.

### 7. Run the four watching-yourself failure-mode check

Quick honesty check. Did you fall into any of the four watching-yourself failure modes from Lecture 2 §4.4?

```
FAILURE MODE CHECK

- The "I sound terrible" spiral:    did you watch all 3 minutes? yes / no
- The fast-forward:                  did you start at 1.0x, not 2x? yes / no
- The polish-over-structure note:    are 2 of 3 notes structural? yes / no
- The "I'll fix it at the event":    is take 2 on the calendar? yes / no
```

A failing failure-mode check is fine — you noted it, you committed it, you fix it for take 2. The check itself is the discipline.

### 8. Commit

```
git add week-06-prep/exercise-02-record-v1.md
git commit -m "week 6 exercise 2: take 1 recorded, four watches, three notes for take 2"
git push
```

The hosted recording link is in the file; the video itself is on the host. Do not commit MP4 files this week.

## Hints

- **Press record before you feel ready.** Most learners spend 30 minutes adjusting their setup before take 1. The setup is checks 1–4; if those pass, press record. Polish is the trap.
- **Time the take with a phone in-shot.** Many event judges record with a visible timer. The visible timer trains the muscle to feel the 3-minute clock.
- **Watch the take in a different room if you can.** A different physical context reduces the urge to spiral. Walk away from your recording desk; watch from the couch.
- **Note the timestamp of each issue.** "0:23 — apology preface" is sharper than "the hook was rough." The timestamp makes the note actionable for take 2.
- **Three notes, not ten.** If you write ten notes, the second take becomes a corrections-performance, not a delivery. Cut the list to three; the other seven are for take 3.

---

## What to do if take 1 is over 4:00

You have three options:

1. **The hook is too long.** Time the hook on the recording. If it is over 35 seconds, cut a line from it. Most over-runs start at the hook.
2. **The solution beat is a feature tour.** Count the clicks. If it is over 4, you are clicking beyond the MUST list. Cut to the 3 MUST verbs.
3. **The problem beat repeats the hook.** Re-watch 0:30–1:15. If the problem beat re-describes the user, that is a repeat; cut to the cost paragraph and the handoff.

Pick the longest beat of the three (hook, problem, solution) and cut it. Do not try to cut all three on take 2; one structural cut is the right size of fix.

---

## What to do if the live URL goes down mid-take

If the deploy URL fails during the recording:

1. **Stop the recording.** This is a technical failure, not a human fluff. Restart is allowed.
2. **Log the URL failure.** Note the timestamp and the symptom (502, blank page, CORS error, timeout). This is also a Week-5 finding — the URL is the bench, and it just failed.
3. **Fix the URL.** Re-deploy if Vercel-style, restart the dyno if Railway-style, check the env vars if Supabase-style.
4. **Re-record.** Run the take again from the start. The earlier take is discarded; the recording is the take that ran end-to-end with a working URL.
5. **Add a Week-5 walk-through note.** The URL-failure scenario is exactly what the pre-event walk-through should have caught. Add a one-line note to your `SCOPING-WORKSHEET.md`'s demo-ability checklist about the failure type.
