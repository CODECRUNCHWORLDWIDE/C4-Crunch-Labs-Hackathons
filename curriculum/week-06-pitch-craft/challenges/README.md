# Week 6 — Challenges

The mini-project is the 3-minute recorded demo of your own Week-3 build. **The challenge is the 45-minute paired drill that tests whether your demo strip works on a build you do not personally own.** It is optional, but if you can find any cohort-mate, non-C4 friend, or sibling with a live URL that works — it is the single best way to see whether your pitch muscle transfers outside the build you already know inside out.

## Index

1. **[Challenge 1 — Pitch someone else's project](challenge-01-pitch-someone-elses-project.md)** — a 45-minute paired drill where you write a 3-minute demo script for your partner's project, record one take of it, and they record one take of yours. (~30 minutes of drill + 15 minutes of debrief)

The challenge is optional. If you skip it, the recorded mini-project still passes Week 6. If you do the drill, you arrive at Week 7 with a calibrated sense of which of the five pitch failure modes you are most prone to *when the build is not yours* — which is exactly the position you will be in at hour 33 of a real event, when the team's build is the team's, not yours alone.

The cross-build constraint is the value. A drill where you pitch your own build re-rehearses what Exercises 1–3 already covered. A drill where you pitch a partner's build forces you to *find the hook* in a build whose story you do not pre-know — and the finding is the muscle.
