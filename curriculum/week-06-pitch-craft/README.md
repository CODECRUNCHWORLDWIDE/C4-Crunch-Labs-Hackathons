# Week 6 — Pitch Craft

Welcome to **Week 6** of **C4 · Crunch Labs Hackathons**. Week 4 gave your team a *contract* — how the team runs. Week 5 gave your team a *worksheet* — what the team ships. Week 6 gives your team a *script* — how the team's three MUST items are walked through, on the live URL, in three minutes, in front of an audience that has 90 seconds of patience and a 30-second attention budget for your hook. The contract is the rhythm; the worksheet is the map; the pitch is the delivery. A team with all three holds the room. A team missing the pitch ships and then loses the demo session it deserved.

If Week 5 was the cut-list rehearsal, Week 6 is the *recording rehearsal*. You will turn the demo-timing strip from BRAND.md into a five-beat three-minute script — hook, problem, solution, demo, ask — and then you will record yourself walking it. You will watch the recording. You will cringe. You will note the three things that broke. You will re-record. The cycle is the lecture; the second recording is the artifact. Most learners arrive at their first hackathon having *thought about* their demo; the ones who win the demo session have *recorded and watched* themselves at least twice.

The deliverable is a **3-minute recorded demo** of your Week-3 solo prototype, fit to the demo-timing strip, uploaded with a public link in your repo. Every following week — and every future event — leans on this script being honest about the 30-second hook, the 15-second ask, and the 90-second solution beat in between. The script is the half of pitch craft you can write; the recording is the half you can only learn by watching yourself.

## Learning objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

- **Defend** the three-minute demo-timing strip (30s hook / 45s problem / 90s solution / 15s ask) as a *bench against which every demo is graded*, not a flexible target. Three minutes is three minutes; over-runs and under-runs both cost the same — judge confidence.
- **Write** a five-beat demo script for any MUST-clean build in under 60 minutes — hook, problem, solution-walkthrough, demo-clip, ask — with a named timestamp at every beat boundary and a named verb at the demo beat.
- **Record** a three-minute demo against the live URL (not localhost, not a screenshot, not a screenshare-of-localhost) in one take, then watch it once, then re-record once. The two-take minimum is the floor; most pitches need three to four.
- **Identify** the five most common pitch failure modes — the buried lede, the feature tour, the apology preface, the silent demo, the missing ask — and the one-sentence fix for each.
- **Run** the 60-second hook test on any demo opener: does the first sentence name a *user* and a *pain*, and does the second sentence promise the *verb* the demo will perform? If not, the hook is decoration; re-write it.
- **Run** the 30-second ask test on any demo closer: does the last 15 seconds name *one* ask (a number, a follow-up, a sign-up, a feedback channel), or does it close with a generic "thanks for watching"? If generic, the ask is missing; re-write it.
- **Watch** your own recording with the demo-strip overlay and grade each beat against the timing band: hook overran by 8 seconds → trim two sentences; demo beat underran by 20 seconds → add a second click; ask was 4 seconds → add a number and a follow-up channel.
- **Commit** a three-minute recorded demo of your Week-3 solo prototype to a public host (YouTube unlisted, Loom public, Vimeo, or a direct MP4 in the repo's `demo/` folder), with the link published in the repo README.

## Prerequisites

- **Week 1 completed.** Your Hackathon Operating Document is committed. The demo-or-die doctrine is familiar; this week extends it from "the demo must work" to "the demo must *land*."
- **Week 2 completed.** Your `hackathon-template` repo is public and `bootstrap.sh` produces a live deploy URL. Week 6 *rehearses on this URL* — if it is broken, Week 6 surfaces it (again, after Week 5 surfaced it).
- **Week 3 completed.** Your solo prototype is deployed and you have a one-paragraph retro. Week 6 *records the demo of that prototype* — the prototype is the demo subject. If your Week-3 build is no longer reachable, re-deploy before Wednesday or pick a different recent build.
- **Week 4 completed.** Your `TEAM-CONTRACT.md` is committed. The contract's "stand-up" rhythm is the same rhythm that holds the pitch script's beats.
- **Week 5 completed.** Your `SCOPING-WORKSHEET.md` is committed. The worksheet's MUST list (3 items) is the spine of the demo script's solution beat (3 verbs, 3 clicks, 90 seconds).
- **A recording setup.** Phone camera, laptop webcam, or screen-recording software. No paid equipment; the constraint is the discipline. Loom free, OBS free, QuickTime free, phone front-camera free — pick one.
- **A quiet 30 minutes for each recording take.** Two takes minimum this week; three to four is typical. Block the time; do not multi-task.

You do not need a team this week. The pitch is solo; the muscle is solo; the recording is solo. If you have a partner from C4 or outside, the optional challenge (Challenge 1) gives them a real role — pitching *their* build under the same strip. The mini-project does not require it.

## Topics covered

- The three-minute demo-timing strip from BRAND.md — five beats, four boundaries, the audience's attention budget at each beat
- The 30-second hook — who is hurting, the punch, the verb the demo will perform; what a strong hook sentence looks like
- The 45-second problem beat — concrete, narrow, specific; the *one* pain the demo solves; the audience's nodding-along test
- The 90-second solution beat — three clicks on the live URL, one MUST verb per click; the demo is the build, not the slide-deck
- The 15-second demo-clip beat — the single most-quotable 15 seconds of the recording, the line judges remember
- The 15-second ask — one number, one channel, one specific next step; not "thanks for watching"
- Recording on the live URL — the bench from Week 5 is also the recording surface; the same "no localhost" rule applies
- Watching the first take — the cringe is the data; the three notes you write while watching are the second-take improvements
- Re-recording — the second take is the artifact; the third take is for the cohort review; the fourth take is for the event itself
- The five pitch failure modes — buried lede, feature tour, apology preface, silent demo, missing ask
- The "first-sentence audit" — read the first sentence aloud; if it does not contain a user and a verb, re-write
- The "last-sentence audit" — read the last sentence aloud; if it does not contain a number and a channel, re-write
- The "no apology" rule — the recording opens with the hook, never with a setup sentence
- The "live URL is the backdrop" rule — the demo beat is filmed against the live URL, not against slides; slides are for follow-up
- The bridge from Week 5's MUST list to Week 6's demo script — three MUSTs become three clicks become 90 seconds of solution beat

## Stretch goals

If you finish the recording on Saturday and want to push further, try any of the following. None substitute for the recording — record the demo first, every time.

- **Take 3 for the cohort review.** The second take is the artifact; a third take, recorded after a 24-hour gap, almost always lands better. The gap is the value — your tongue forgets the script enough to deliver it, not recite it.
- **Trade recordings with another C4 learner.** Watch theirs at 1.0x. Note one beat where their pacing was better than yours. The pitch you would record at a real event is the union of three good demos you watched, not the first take you made alone.
- **Find a public hackathon demo video with a strong opener.** Watch the first 30 seconds. Pause. Write down the hook sentence verbatim. Note what made it work: a named user, a named pain, a verb. That is the muscle most beginners skip in their first take — copy the shape, not the wording.
- **Re-read your Week 3 retro alongside Lecture 1 §3 (the problem beat).** The pain you named in your Week-3 prompt is the pain your demo's problem beat names. If the two do not match, the prompt or the demo is misaligned — re-align by re-recording, not by editing the prompt.
- **Record one 60-second elevator version of the same demo.** Same MUST list, same live URL, one minute flat. The compression is the discipline. The elevator version is what you say to a sponsor at the event hallway when you have not earned three minutes yet.
