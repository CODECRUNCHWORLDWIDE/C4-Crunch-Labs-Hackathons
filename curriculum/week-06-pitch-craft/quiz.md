## Answer key

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

1. **B** — 0:00 – 0:30 (30 seconds). The hook is capped at 30 seconds in the BRAND.md demo-timing strip. (Lecture 1 §1.2 and BRAND.md)
2. **C** — A user and a pain. The first-sentence audit grades any hook in 30 seconds: does sentence 1 name a user AND a pain? (Lecture 1 §2.1)
3. **B** — Three clicks on the live URL, one MUST verb per click. The MUST list from Week 5 *is* the solution beat's three clicks. (Lecture 1 §4.1)
4. **B** — A specific number, a channel, and a next step. The ask is 15 seconds; "thanks for watching" lacks all three components. (Lecture 1 §5.1 and §5.2)
5. **C** — The over-styled font is not a failure mode. The five named modes are: buried lede, feature tour, apology preface, silent demo, missing ask. (Lecture 1 §6)
6. **B** — 3 to 4 takes. The two-take minimum is the floor; most pitches need 3 to 4 takes, and the 24-hour gap between takes is where the delivery sharpens. (Lecture 2 §1.2)
7. **A** — The four watches catch four different layers: pacing (1.0x audio+video), structural drift (1.5x audio+video), visual gaps (audio off), and verbal gaps (video off). (Lecture 2 §4.1)
8. **B** — Three notes after the four watches; three changes is the right budget for take 2 to land without becoming stiff. (Lecture 2 §4.3)
9. **B** — A slide deck of screenshots of the build is excluded. Screenshots are not clicks; the solution beat is filmed against the live URL. (Lecture 2 §2.2 and Lecture 1 §4.3)
10. **C** — A 3-minute recorded demo of the Week-3 solo prototype, hosted publicly, linked in the repo README, with take count noted. The transparency rule is the C4 voice's honesty discipline. (Lecture 2 §9.1 and the mini-project README)
