# Week 5 — Exercises

Three short rehearsals, done Tuesday through Friday. None of them require teammates — though all three are sharper with a partner. They are the dry-runs of the three muscles you bring to the next event: the demo-ability grade on a past build, the 20-minute MoSCoW pass under a clock, and the pre-written cut-list for the three predictable moments.

## Index

1. **[Exercise 1 — Rate your prior builds](exercise-01-rate-your-prior-builds.md)** — grade your Week 3 solo build (and any past hackathon work you have) against the five-question demo-ability test. (~60 min, Tuesday)
2. **[Exercise 2 — Scope stress game](exercise-02-scope-stress-game.md)** — run a 20-minute MoSCoW pass on a fake event prompt; force MUST to 3; commit. (~60 min, Thursday)
3. **[Exercise 3 — Cut-list rehearsal](exercise-03-cut-list-rehearsal.md)** — pre-write the three named cuts for hour 2, hour 12, hour 24 against the fake prompt from Exercise 2. (~60 min, Friday)

## How to work the exercises

- Read the prompt. Skim, do not memorize.
- **Type or write the answer yourself.** Do not paste a giant snippet and edit. The point is muscle memory.
- Use the C4 voice: team-room, blameless, scope-discipline-first. No "rockstar" / "ninja" / "10x." Even when you are alone.
- Commit each completed exercise to the *Week 5 prep* folder of your `hackathon-template` repo (or a new `c4-week-05-prep-<yourhandle>` repo). Small commits.
- If you get stuck for more than 15 minutes, re-read the lecture section the exercise references, *then* try again.

The three drills are short on purpose. The skill is built in the dry-run, not in the writing. Run each exercise against a *real clock* — the 20-minute MoSCoW pass and the demo-ability grade both have hard timeboxes. Honor them.

These exercises are the difference between a scoping worksheet written in theory and a scoping worksheet written from real reps. Do not skip them.
