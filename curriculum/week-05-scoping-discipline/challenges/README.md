# Week 5 — Challenges

The mini-project is the 36-hour scoping worksheet template. **The challenge is the 30-minute paired drill that tests whether your MoSCoW pass works on a build you do not personally own.** It is optional, but if you can find any non-C4 friend with a half-formed idea — a startup, a class project, a side-project, a club initiative — it is the single best way to see whether your scoping muscle transfers outside the fake prompts of Exercises 1–3.

## Index

1. **[Challenge 1 — Scope a friend's idea](challenge-01-scope-a-friends-idea.md)** — a 30-minute paired exercise where you run the 20-minute MoSCoW pass on a non-C4 partner's real idea, then write a 3-paragraph cut-list. (~30 minutes of drill + 15 minutes of debrief)

The challenge is optional. If you skip it, the worksheet still passes Week 5. If you do the drill, you arrive at Week 6 with a calibrated sense of which of the four MoSCoW failure modes you are most prone to *when the idea is not yours* — which is exactly the position you will be in at hour 0 of a real event, when the team's prompt is the team's, not yours alone.

The non-C4 partner constraint is the value. A drill with another C4 learner re-rehearses material you both already share. A drill with a non-C4 friend forces you to *teach* the framework as you apply it — and the teaching is the muscle.
