# C4 · Crunch Labs — Hackathons · Syllabus

**10 weeks · ~10 hours/week · ~100 hours total · Hackathon-ready by end of cycle.**

A short, opinionated prep course designed to take a learner from "I've heard of hackathons" to a confident contributor at one in 10 weeks. Pairs naturally with a real upcoming event — the capstone is the event itself.

---

## Program at a glance

| Phase | Weeks | Outcome |
|-------|-------|---------|
| **Phase 1 — Foundations** | 01 – 03 | Toolkit set up, first solo prototype, Agile vocabulary |
| **Phase 2 — Team Mechanics** | 04 – 06 | Sprint cadence, scoping discipline, pitch craft |
| **Phase 3 — Pre-Event** | 07 – 09 | Stack rehearsal, dry-run sprint, demo polish |
| **Phase 4 — Event + Post** | 10 | Real hackathon + post-mortem |

---

## Weekly breakdown

### Phase 1 — Foundations

**Week 1 — The Hackathon Mental Model.** What hackathons actually reward (problem clarity > tech depth > polish). The 12-hour rule. The "demo or die" doctrine. Picking your role: PM, full-stack, designer, pitch lead.

- *Mini-deliverable:* A 1-page personal brief — your strengths, your default role, your stack preference.

**Week 2 — Rapid-prototyping toolkit setup.** Install the team's stack: a frontend (React + Vite or Next), a backend (FastAPI or Express), a DB (SQLite or Supabase free), a deploy target (Vercel or Railway free tier). Pre-tune the dev environment so the first 6 hours of an event aren't yak-shaving.

- *Mini-deliverable:* A `hackathon-template` repo on your GitHub. One command to scaffold a new project.

**Week 3 — Solo prototype sprint.** A 6-hour solo build of *anything*: a todo app with one creative twist, a weather widget, a meme generator. The point is to feel the rhythm.

- *Mini-deliverable:* Live-deployed solo prototype.

### Phase 2 — Team Mechanics

**Week 4 — Agile ceremonies, real-world version.** Stand-up, demo, retro — what each is *for* and how to compress them to fit 36 hours. Story-pointing under time pressure. Saying "no" to scope creep.

- *Mini-deliverable:* A 1-page team contract template for the next event.

**Week 5 — Scoping discipline.** The single skill that separates winning teams from "we ran out of time." MoSCoW (Must, Should, Could, Won't), the cut-list, the demo-ability test. *What do we cut first?* must be answered hour 1.

- *Mini-deliverable:* A 36-hour scoping worksheet you'll bring to the event.

**Week 6 — Pitch craft.** Three-minute demo structure: hook (30s) → problem (45s) → solution (90s) → demo (15s clip). Recording yourself. Watching, cringing, re-recording.

- *Mini-deliverable:* A 3-minute recorded demo of your Week-3 solo prototype.

### Phase 3 — Pre-Event

**Week 7 — Team-mode dry-run, day 1.** With your real teammates (or a peer simulating), a 6-hour mock hackathon. Same ceremonies, same scoping, half the time.

- *Mini-deliverable:* The mock project, deployed.

**Week 8 — Team-mode dry-run, day 2.** A second 6-hour run on a fresh prompt. Different team if possible. The point is to feel the team negotiation, not the tech.

- *Mini-deliverable:* Written retro on team dynamics. What worked? What blew up?

**Week 9 — Event recon.** Read the upcoming event's sponsors, prizes, judging rubric. Identify the unfair advantages — which sponsor APIs are easy wins, which prizes have least competition. Pre-plan your scoping cut-list given the rubric.

- *Mini-deliverable:* Event brief: sponsors → likely tools → strategic prize targets → opening-hour roles.

### Phase 4 — Event + Post

**Week 10 — The event + post-mortem.** You attend a real hackathon. Apply everything. Submit. Then within 72 hours, write a structured post-mortem.

- *Capstone:* Submitted project (public repo + live demo URL) + 3-minute recorded demo + written post-mortem.

## How the weekly load adds up

| Component | hrs/wk |
|-----------|------:|
| Lecture / reading | 2 |
| Hands-on prototyping | 4 |
| Team practice / mock sprint | 2 |
| Pitch / demo recording | 1 |
| Reflection / journaling | 1 |
| **Total** | **10** |

---

## Capstone rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|-----------|------:|
| Project actually deployed and reachable from a public URL at the time of judging | 25% |
| Demo video lands the 3-minute structure | 20% |
| Scoping log shows deliberate cuts (not failure-by-default) | 15% |
| Public repo with a real README a stranger could run | 15% |
| Post-mortem identifies 3 specific behaviors to change for the next event | 15% |
| Team contract was used during the event | 10% |
