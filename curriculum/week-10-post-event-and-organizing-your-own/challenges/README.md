# Week 10 — Challenges

Two optional stretch challenges. Both are listed in the README's "How to navigate this week" table. Neither is required to pass Week 10; both are listed by C4 alumni as the assignment that *most accelerated their organising readiness* after the track.

| File | What it asks | Time-box | Re-usable by the capstone |
|------|--------------|----------|---------------------------|
| [challenge-01-attendee-survey.md](./challenge-01-attendee-survey.md) | Design the post-event attendee survey for the capstone | 60–90 min | Yes — replaces the default in `post-event/attendee-survey.md` |
| [challenge-02-sponsor-deck.md](./challenge-02-sponsor-deck.md) | Draft a 5–8 slide sponsorship deck PDF that pairs with the Exercise 3 cold-emails | 90–120 min | Yes — committed to `sponsorship/deck-template.pdf` |

## Pick one, pick both, or skip both

- **Pick one if** your Sunday has 60–120 minutes of remaining bandwidth after the capstone first draft is committed. Challenge 1 (the survey) takes the shorter slot; Challenge 2 (the deck) takes the longer slot.
- **Pick both if** you intend to run the capstone live within 12 months. The two challenges together produce two of the *four* live-event artifacts that the capstone plan otherwise references-as-templates rather than instantiates.
- **Skip both if** the capstone first draft is at 80 percent at Sunday evening. The capstone is the binding artifact; the challenges are the polish.

## The C4 voice on challenges

Challenges are *named optionality*. The track's commitment to learners is that the structured target (lectures + exercises + quiz + homework + mini-project) is achievable in the ~17-hour structured budget. The challenges are the *if you want to push further* layer. C4 alumni surveys consistently flag Challenge 2 (the sponsorship deck) as the single most-leveraged stretch artifact in the entire 10-week track — sponsors *forward* a deck inside their company in a way they do not forward a cold email alone. If you can only pick one and you intend to actually pitch sponsors in the next 6 months, pick Challenge 2.

## Reference loop

Both challenges link back into the mini-project's folder structure:

```text
mini-project/
├── post-event/
│   └── attendee-survey.md     ← Challenge 1 lands here
└── sponsorship/
    └── deck-template.pdf      ← Challenge 2 lands here
```

The mini-project's acceptance criteria treat both files as *optional* — a default in their place is acceptable for the structured-target path. Stretch acceptance treats both files as *required* — the live-event-ready capstone has both committed.
