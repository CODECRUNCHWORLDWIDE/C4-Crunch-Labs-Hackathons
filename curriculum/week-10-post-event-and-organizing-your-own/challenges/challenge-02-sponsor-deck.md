# Challenge 2 — Draft a 5–8 Slide Sponsorship Deck

## Goal

Draft a 5–8 slide sponsorship deck PDF that pairs with the Exercise 3 cold-emails. The deck is what the email *attaches to*; the deck is what gets *forwarded inside the sponsor company*; the deck is what *closes deals the email opens*. Without a deck, the cold-email is a *naked ask*; with a deck, the cold-email is a *proposal*.

## Time-box

90–120 minutes.

## Reading

- Lecture 3 §1 (the cold-email template) and §4 (sponsor liability and the MOU).
- Lecture 2 §2 (the org-side six-track structure — most slides draw on these).
- MLH Organizer Guide — sponsors section, for the named *tier menu* structures.

## Constraints

- **Page count.** 5–8 slides. Fewer is thin; more is presumptuous for a first-event ask.
- **Format.** PDF only. Do not send a Google Slides link; sponsors forward PDFs to internal stakeholders, and Google Slides links break across orgs.
- **Density.** Each slide is *one idea*. Title + sub-title + visual + one paragraph of body. No slide should require the sponsor to read more than 30 seconds.
- **Branding.** The event's own logo + event website at the bottom of each slide. The sponsor's logo *not* on the deck before they have agreed to be on the deck.

## Slide-by-slide structure (the named C4 default)

### Slide 1 — Event one-pager

- Title: `<Event Name>` and the tagline.
- Sub-title: date, venue, attendee count, format (one-day or 24-hour).
- Visual: a hero photo from a past event (or, for a first event, a campus photo).
- Body: 2 sentences — what the event is and why it matters.

### Slide 2 — Audience demographics

- Title: *Who attends*.
- Visual: a pie chart of majors, a bar of years, a line of geographic draw.
- Body: 3 bullets — major split, year split, geographic draw.

### Slide 3 — The tier menu

- Title: *Sponsorship tiers*.
- Visual: a 4-column table — Bronze / Silver / Gold / Title.
- Body: dollar range + named deliverables per tier.

### Slide 4 — Past-event recap (or peer-event recap)

- Title: *Recap*.
- Visual: 3–4 photos from a past event (or, for a first event, photos from a peer event at a comparable campus, attributed).
- Body: 2 bullets — what previous sponsors got out of past events.

### Slide 5 — Sponsor logo placement (mock-ups)

- Title: *Where your logo appears*.
- Visual: a mock T-shirt with sponsor logos, a mock website screenshot with sponsor logos, a mock closing-ceremony slide with sponsor logos.
- Body: 1 sentence — what each tier sees in terms of placement.

### Slide 6 — The ask

- Title: *Join as a <tier> sponsor*.
- Visual: a clean call-to-action — the tier, the dollar amount, the contact email.
- Body: 1 sentence — the specific ask. *This slide changes per sponsor*; the deck is *not one-size-fits-all*; customise the ask slide for each cold email.

### Slide 7 — Organising team and contact

- Title: *The team*.
- Visual: 3–5 organiser headshots with names + roles.
- Body: contact email + a one-sentence "we reply within 48 hours."

### Slide 8 (optional) — Appendix

- Title: *Appendix*.
- Body: code of conduct link, insurance summary (named coverage limits), MLH affiliation (if applicable), past sponsor list (if applicable).

## Tools

- **Canva — free tier.** Slide templates abound. Export to PDF.
- **Figma — free tier.** More flexible; export each frame as a PDF page.
- **Google Slides — export to PDF.** Acceptable but requires manual export.
- **Keynote / PowerPoint — local export to PDF.** Acceptable.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] 5–8 slides, exactly.
- [ ] Exported as a PDF.
- [ ] Each slide has one idea (title + sub-title + visual + ≤30 seconds of body).
- [ ] Event logo and website on every slide.
- [ ] Sponsor logos *not* on the deck before they have agreed.
- [ ] The "ask" slide is customisable per sponsor (note the placeholder in the file).
- [ ] Deck file size under 5 MB (smaller PDFs forward more reliably).
