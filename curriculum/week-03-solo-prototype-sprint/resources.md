# Week 3 — Resources

Every resource on this page is **free** and **publicly accessible**. No paywalled books, no signups beyond the free tier. If a link breaks, please open an issue.

## Required reading (work it into your week)

- **MoSCoW method (Wikipedia).** A four-minute refresh; you saw this in Week 1. Re-read §"Categories" before Tuesday: <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MoSCoW_method>
- **Atlassian — Sprint retrospectives.** Skim the "keep / start / stop" and "what went well / what didn't / actions" sections; ignore the corporate scaling chatter: <https://www.atlassian.com/agile/scrum/retrospectives>
- **OBS Studio — Quickstart.** You will record a three-minute demo. Install Wednesday, do not wait until Saturday: <https://obsproject.com/wiki/OBS-Studio-Quickstart>

If you only have time for one, install OBS. The recording is the artifact future-you will reach for at every event.

## Recording the three-minute demo

| Tool | Why | Link |

## Free retro templates

- **Atlassian retro play — Sprint retrospective.** A copyable structure: <https://www.atlassian.com/team-playbook/plays/retrospective>
- **Retromat.** A free generator of retro formats; ignore the marketing copy and steal the prompts: <https://retromat.org/>

C4 does not require any specific retro template. Lecture 2 §4 gives you a one-page solo-retro shape; if it does not fit your build, swap it.

## On the six-hour clock

- **"Speed of Iteration Beats Quality of Iteration" — Karpathy.** A short blog-style note; the phrase is the value, the elaboration is bonus: <https://karpathy.ai/>
- **"Boring Idea, Demoed Flawlessly" — C4 Week 1 §4 recap.** Re-read the four reasons novel ideas underperform. Solo builds are the smallest unit of test for that hypothesis.
- **The "12-hour rule" applied to a 6-hour build.** Cut the midpoint check in half: at hour three, your remaining scope must fit in three hours plus a 30% buffer, or you cut. Same rule, smaller window.

## Glossary cheat sheet

| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Solo build** | A timed, single-person build run as a rehearsal for team events. The point is the rhythm, not the output. |
| **Demo or die** | If it is not in the three-minute demo, it does not exist. Applied to a solo build, the URL must be live at hour six, period. |
| **The cut reflex** | The hourly habit of asking "what do I cut to still ship?" out loud. You answer it even if the answer is "nothing yet." |
| **MUST (in MoSCoW)** | On a six-hour solo build, MUST is **one** feature you can demo. Not three. One. |
| **The hour-five freeze** | At hour 5:00, you stop writing new code. The last hour is for the demo path, polish, recording, and the retro draft. |
| **Keep / Start / Stop** | The minimum viable retro: three columns, one to four bullets each, brutally honest. |
| **The three-behavior change list** | The retro's payload: three specific behaviors — not goals, not vibes — that you will change at the next build. |
| **Blameless retro** | "The sprint board didn't catch the rate limit" — not "I screwed up the rate limit." Voice applied to yourself. |
