# Week 1 — Resources

Every resource on this page is **free** and **publicly accessible**. No paywalled books, no signups. If a link breaks, please open an issue.

## Required reading (work it into your week)

- **Major League Hacking — Guide to Your First Hackathon.** The single most useful free document for newcomers. Skim Monday, re-read Friday: <https://hackp.ac/mlh-guidebook>
- **MLH — What is a hackathon?** A two-minute primer if the event format itself is new to you: <https://mlh.io/about>
- **Devpost — Hackathon judging criteria explainer.** Read what *judges* are actually told to look for: <https://help.devpost.com/hc/en-us/articles/360021955451-Judging-and-Winners>
- **MoSCoW method — Wikipedia.** The cleanest open reference for the framework C4 leans on heaviest: <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MoSCoW_method>

## Glossary cheat sheet

Keep this open in a tab when reading Lecture 1 and 2.

| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Hackathon** | A time-boxed event (usually 24–48 hours) where teams build a small project from scratch and demo it to judges |
| **Sprint Coral** | The C4 brand accent color; on a sprint board, a "must-ship" card is Sprint Coral |
| **MoSCoW** | A prioritization technique: **Must**, **Should**, **Could**, **Won't** |
| **Demo-or-die** | If a feature does not appear in the three-minute demo, it does not exist in the judges' eyes |
| **The 12-hour rule** | At hour 12 of a 36-hour event, the remaining scope must fit in the remaining time *plus a 30% buffer*, or you cut |
| **Cut reflex** | The team habit of asking "what do we cut next?" hourly, not at the deadline |
| **Demo URL** | The publicly reachable URL judges can click during pitches (Vercel, Railway, Fly free tier, etc.) |
| **Devpost** | The default hackathon-submission platform; where most events host their pages and where winners are listed |
| **MLH** | Major League Hacking, the league that sanctions most North American college hackathons |
| **Sponsor prize** | A side prize offered by a sponsor (often for using their API). Often easier to win than the grand prize |
| **Pitch lead** | The role responsible for the three-minute demo. Not always the team's "best engineer" |
| **Post-mortem** | A blameless write-up after the event identifying what to do differently next time |
