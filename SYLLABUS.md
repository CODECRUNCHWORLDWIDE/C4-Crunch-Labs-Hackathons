# C4 · Crunch Labs — Hackathons — Syllabus

**Format:** 10 weeks · self-paced · open-source (GPL-3.0). Each week = lectures, exercises, challenges, a mini-project, homework, and a quiz.

**Prerequisites:** C1 Weeks 1–7 (or equivalent Python), and you can branch, push, and open/merge a PR on GitHub. Some web exposure (you know what an HTTP request is). Willingness to attend a real hackathon — the capstone is event-driven.

**Assessment (honor-based):** tick each lesson complete in the reader; finish the weekly mini-project and quiz. A certificate is issued on 100% completion to One-Stop members.

| Week | Theme | Mini-project |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1 | The hackathon mental model — what events reward, picking your role | Your hackathon operating document |
| 2 | Rapid-prototyping toolkit — frontend + backend + DB + deploy, pre-tuned | Your `hackathon-template` repo, polished |
| 3 | Solo prototype sprint — feel the six-hour rhythm | Six-hour solo build + recorded demo + retro |
| 4 | Agile ceremonies, real-world version — standup, demo, retro in 36 hrs | The one-page team contract template |
| 5 | Scoping discipline — MoSCoW, the cut-list, demo-ability | The 36-hour scoping worksheet template |
| 6 | Pitch craft — the 3-minute demo structure | The 3-minute recorded demo |
| 7 | Team-mode dry-run, day 1 — a mock event with real teammates | A 4-hour mock hackathon at home |
| 8 | Team-mode dry-run, day 2 — hours 12 to 24 | The hours-12-to-24 continuation |
| 9 | Judging, scoring, and reading the room | The mock pitch and mock Q&A |
| 10 | Post-event carry-out and organizing your own | Organise a one-day campus hackathon (capstone) |

**Outcome:** ship a scoped project under deadline pressure on a team of strangers, pitch it in three minutes, and run the post-mortem that improves your next event.
